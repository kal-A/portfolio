# Artifact Reality and Project Treatment

Very important: different projects have different levels of physical/visual
artifacts. Do not treat them all the same. Do not fake artifacts for any
project.

## Artifact strength ranking (work projects)

Use this order when deciding how visually rich each work project should be:

1. **Greenhouse** — most physical/design artifacts. Strongest visual/design
   evidence. Best for showing design production, campaigns, brand/e-commerce
   assets, and execution. (Does not have to be the top-featured case study
   for PM-strategy purposes, but is the strongest *design* evidence.)
2. **Hera Fertility** — strong artifact-backed product/design work. Good for
   startup UX/UI, product thinking, onboarding, analytics/dashboard material
   if available.
3. **PathPeer** — some product/design experience, but less physical material.
   Do not over-feature as a visual case study yet. Better as
   supporting/archive work unless more artifacts are added.
4. **Informatica** — least visual/product-design artifact strength among
   these. Do not present as a major visual design case study. Better as
   supporting/archive or process/content/education systems work.

## Public-safe process projects

**ForceN** has no public physical artifacts available. Do not fake artifacts
for ForceN. Instead, present it as a public-safe systems/process/product
engineering case study using:

- reconstructed workflow diagram
- public-safe process map
- before/after handoff table
- anonymized tracker structure
- confidentiality note
- artifact chip saying "Internal materials omitted"

**Informatica** may also need a lighter/supporting treatment unless good
artifacts are available.

## Three project treatment categories

### A. Visual case studies

Use for: **RoomEase, Greenhouse, Hera Fertility, HCI / Pill Pal**

These should show visual/product/design ability. Include where available:
screenshots, Figma frames, flows, wireframes, mockups, campaign assets,
dashboards, diagrams, prototypes, before/after design iterations,
research/evaluation findings, design rationale.

These should **not** be reduced to text-only process summaries.

### B. Public-safe systems/process case studies

Use for: **ForceN**, possibly **Informatica** depending on available material.

These should still look designed, but should not pretend to have public
screenshots. Use: reconstructed diagrams, workflow maps, process stages,
before/after tables, constraints sections, confidentiality notes.

### C. Supporting/archive project cards

Use for: **PathPeer, Informatica**, smaller school projects, smaller personal
projects, job-hunt-related work if relevant.

These should be concise and honest. Do not over-feature them. Do not create
fake visuals. Do not inflate impact.

## PathPeer handling

Do not make PathPeer a major featured visual case study yet — it has less
physical/artifact-backed material compared with Greenhouse, Hera, RoomEase,
and HCI/Pill Pal. Use it in the Project Archive / Supporting Work section.

PathPeer card should include: product/design support, student platform UX,
role, tools, what was helped with, and a content gap note if artifacts are
missing.

Do not: create a large visual case study unless artifacts are later added,
fake screenshots, inflate metrics, make it seem more artifact-backed than it
is.

## Informatica handling

Do not make Informatica a major visual design case study — it doesn't have
the same artifact strength as Greenhouse or Hera. Use it as: supporting/archive
work, education/content/product systems work, possibly public-safe
process/documentation work.

Do not: over-feature it, present it as a strong UX/UI case study unless real
artifacts support that, invent visuals.
