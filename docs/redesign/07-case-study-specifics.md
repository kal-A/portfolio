# Case Study Specifics (Per Project)

## ForceN

Create a ForceN case study without using private screenshots or internal
company materials.

**Important:** No public artifacts, screenshots, or physical documents from
ForceN are currently available to add to the portfolio.

So:
- Do not fake artifacts
- Do not create fake internal dashboards
- Do not imply screenshots are available
- Do not expose confidential company details

Instead, make this a public-safe systems/process case study. Focus on:
product engineering, production workflow, hardware operations, tracking and
handoffs, procurement → assembly → calibration → shipment, coordinating
across engineering, operations, and inventory, turning scattered information
into a clearer process.

**Suggested title:** ForceN — Product Engineering Co-op

**Suggested subtitle:** Turning production handoffs into a trackable system.

**Suggested summary:** ForceN's dev kit workflow touched procurement,
assembly, calibration, inventory, and shipment. I helped make that process
easier to track by organizing unit status, clarifying handoffs, and
coordinating across engineering and operations so fewer details lived only in
people's heads.

**Suggested sections:**
1. Context
2. The messy workflow
3. What I owned
4. How I structured the process
5. Public-safe workflow diagram
6. What changed
7. What I learned
8. Confidentiality note

**Confidentiality note (use verbatim or close to it):** Internal tracking
sheets, tools, and company-specific materials are omitted for confidentiality.
This case study uses a reconstructed public-safe workflow to explain the
product operations and systems thinking behind the work.

Do not use unsupported metrics.

## RoomEase

Should be one of the strongest case studies.

Frame it as: RoomEase was not just a room booking UI. It was an attempt to
make a scattered student club booking process more structured, transparent,
and usable.

**Show:** problem context, requirements gathering, student
organization/stakeholder input, room dataset, booking flow, admin flow, Figma
screens, architecture/process diagrams, final report/presentation/poster,
constraints, what was implemented vs. planned, what to improve next.

Be honest about implementation status — do not pretend it was a fully shipped
production app if it was a capstone prototype/system design.

## Greenhouse

retail/storefront or digital channel design, sprint/campaign coordination

assets.

**Possible case study angle:** Greenhouse — Design Production Across Retail
and Digital Channels

**Possible subtitle:** Shipping campaign and storefront assets across a
growing juice brand.

**Focus on:** what assets were created or updated, how work moved through
campaigns/sprints, how designs supported retail/e-commerce channels, how

design production. But do show: polish, visual design, execution volume,
working across channels, coordination.
