# Selected Case Studies

The selected case studies should balance product, design, systems, and
evidence. Recommended five, in this order:

## 1. RoomEase

- **Type:** Product design / systems design / capstone
- **Why:** Strong school/product/design case study with artifacts.
- **Treatment:** Visual, artifact-rich.
- **Show:** Figma, booking flow, admin flow, dataset, architecture,
  requirements gathering, capstone report/presentation.

## 2. Hera Fertility

- **Type:** Startup UX/UI / product design / analytics/product work
- **Why:** Strong work/project artifact-backed case study.
- **Treatment:** Visual and product-focused.
- **Show:** UX/UI artifacts, onboarding/product work, dashboards or analytics
  if available, Figma/assets where available, product thinking.

## 3. Greenhouse

- **Type:** Design production / brand/e-commerce / campaign execution /
  product-ops-adjacent design
- **Why:** The work experience with the most physical/design artifacts.
- **Treatment:** Design-forward and visual.
- **Important:** Greenhouse doesn't have to be framed as the most
  strategically impressive PM case study, but should be used strongly to
  demonstrate visual design ability, execution, polish, and artifact-backed
  design work.
- **Show:** design assets, campaign materials, storefront/e-commerce assets,
  brand/design production, sprint/campaign coordination where relevant,
  visual design taste.

## 4. HCI / Pill Pal

- **Type:** UX research / wearable UX / usability evaluation
- **Why:** Strong UX/UI and research case study.
- **Treatment:** Design-process heavy and visual.
- **Show:** Apple Watch prototype, cognitive walkthrough, heuristic
  evaluation, usability issues, design iterations, HCI methods.

## 5. ForceN

- **Type:** Product engineering / systems / operations / TPM
- **Why:** Strong work/systems story, but not visual-artifact-backed.
- **Treatment:** Public-safe process case study.
- **Show:** reconstructed workflow, process map, handoff table,
  confidentiality note, no fake artifacts.
